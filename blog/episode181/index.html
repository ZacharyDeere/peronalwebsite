My One Piece Blog:

*SPOILER WARNING*

Episode 181:

Ambition Toward the Endless Vearth! The Ark Maxim!

Summary
At the very beginning Wyper decides to use the sea prism stone shooting Eneru and himself forcefully in opposite directions. Wyper lands on his feet while Eneru lays on the ground presumably dead. Eneru shortly after uses his devil fruit power to restart his heart. With dismay Wyper falls to his knees in shock and in pain while Eneru begins to stand back up. Eneru electrocutes Wyper once again however Wyper still continues to stand back up. Wyper expresses that he continues to fight due to his ancestors’ struggle. Eneru follows up by electrocuting him one last time, a shock so large that it looks like might have killed Wyper. In fury Zoro stands and picks up the sea prism stone and charges straight for Eneru. Just as he did with Wyper, Eneru electrocutes Zoro leaving him charred and unconscious.

At this point, Nami, who had been hiding during the fight, yells out and Eneru informs her that she is the last one still alive. Rather than trying to fight Eneru she decides to join him on his journey. However, it is very apparent that she is trying to escape death. Eneru escorts Nami to his ship that he claims can only be controlled by lightning and is made of out the gold that he stole from the Upper Yard.

Monkey D. Luffy and Aisa finally make it to the Upper Yard and sees all of their friends charred and unconscious. Robin finally come to and is able to relay to Luffy that Eneru and Nami went destroy Skypiea. Aisa explains the she knows exactly where they went.

My Thoughts
It is so upsetting that Luffy took forever to finally reach the Upper Yard. Remember that since Luffy has the powers of the Gum-Gum fruit, he is made of rubber. Therefore, I think it is pretty safe to assume that Eneru’s electric powers would be obsolete when it comes to Luffy because he is literally an insulator. With that being said though I have a feeling that Eneru’s days are numbered once Luffy finally finds him.

The episode seemed to keep the action going much better than episode 180 did. Flashbacks were still intermixed with the on going action but they did not intrude on important scenes.
