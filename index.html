About Me:

Hello, my name is Zachary Deere. I am a Computer Science undergraduate at Angelo State University. Throughout my years at Angelo State I have learned many programming languages. I am most fluent in C++ and Java, however I also know: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Assembly, PHP, Python, and Angelo State's RAMM assembly language.

I am a student of many interests and have found myself in many different fields of work until I realized my love for computers. I spent my high school life working at a fast food restaurant then doubled up on jobs during the summer on the family farm shredding CRP for my grandparents. In college I worked a retail job and went home during the summers to work in the oil field for the next school year's tuition.

The most rewarding and fulfilling occupation is the one I go back to everyone summer back in my hometown. I am a production tubing inspector and tubing technician for Musslewhite Trucking. In short, I inspect production tubing that is ready to be sent downhole to pump oil out of the ground. When the joints of tubing are being connected and put downhole it is my responsibility to insure that each connection is fitted with the correct sized WedgeRing® and to make sure that each connection is tightened perfectly. If the wrong size WedgeRing® is used or the connection is too tight or too loose the integrity of the well may be compromised.
